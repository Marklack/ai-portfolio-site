About

Executive AI leadership,
hands-on and grounded.

I am an executive AI and data leader with deep hands-on experience designing and deploying enterprise-grade AI systems. My work sits at the intersection of data engineering, applied machine learning, and business strategy—translating ambiguous organizational problems into systems that reliably deliver value.

Unlike teams that treat AI as a feature, I build it as infrastructure: modular, observable, and tightly integrated with the workflows people already use. I've led cross-functional efforts spanning data platform architecture, LLM integrations, RAG pipelines, and AI agent frameworks—always with a focus on practical outcomes over technical novelty.

I'm skeptical of AI hype and grounded in results. Whether the challenge is improving data quality, shortening decision cycles, or scaling intelligent automation, my north star is measurable business impact—not model benchmarks.

Philosophy

How I Think About AI

Most AI systems fail not because the models are wrong—but because they are disconnected from real data and real workflows.

Grounded in Real Data

I start with the data: where it lives, how it moves, and what decisions it should inform. Disconnected AI is the most common cause of enterprise AI failure.

Decision Support Over Novelty

I build AI that helps people make better decisions faster. That means usability, reliability, and clear feedback loops—not state-of-the-art benchmarks.

Governance & Measurable Impact

Enterprise AI must be auditable, observable, and safe. I build governance into the architecture—and define success in terms of measurable business outcomes.

Systems Thinking

The model is one component. The retrieval pipeline, the data quality layer, the workflow integration, the feedback mechanism—these determine production success.
